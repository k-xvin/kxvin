# The Bobamabob, V1

It's a robot that makes boba!  You can find a video of it in action at https://bobamabob.com/.

![bobamabob_v1.jpeg](/content/attachments/bobamabob_v1.jpeg)

It took about 6 months of outside-of-job time, starting from almost no knowledge of how to put a robot together. Plus, we kind of built two copies of this robot since my friend and I live in different states.

Our original idea of a hamburger-making robot was "too hard." That didn't make this boba robot any easier. Not sure why all our ideas were food robots.

I learned:
* how to make a gantry
	* timing belt and stepper motor
* controlling stepper motors
* controlling a gantry with pathfinding firmware
* using aluminum extrusions
* cutting aluminum extrusions with a mitre saw
* pumping and managing liquids in a robot
* designing numerous custom parts using FreeCAD and 3D printing the parts out
* using epoxy
* learning about food safety
* how to network with people
* talking to other people about robotics
* setting up and managing a booth at a convention
* talking to people nonstop at a convention is very tiring and tiring to the voice. if you find yourself in this situation, continuously drink water!!
* the environment you build something in greatly contributes to your motivation to work on that thing
* connecting with people on linkedin and networking
* working with different DC voltages
* debugging power supply errors caused by mysterious short circuits (thanks michael! you know who you are)
* how to make a big sugary, squelchy mess inside of a robot
